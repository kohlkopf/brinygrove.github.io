Sea-to-soil circularity · Pacific Northwest rooted

Regenerative hazelnuts and truffles near Washington's Puget Sound.

Briny Grove Farmstead is a 10-acre regenerative orchard in Washington State where hazelnut trees double as the inoculated hosts for Périgord black truffles. The farm is built around a circular, sea-to-soil philosophy that transforms waste oyster shells from Puget Sound into a valuable soil input—closing loops, reducing waste, and creating a Puget Sound-influenced terroir.

Orchard Symbiosis Vision

Hazelnut + truffle orchard

A resilient, dual-purpose planting crafted for long-term canopy health, fungal vigor, and Puget Sound terroir.

Hazelnuts

Cold-hardy European hazelnuts selected for disease resistance, productivity, and flavor—laid out for stable root zones, runoff control, and fungal health, and inoculated to host Périgord truffles.

Périgord truffles

Tuber melanosporum nurtured beneath the very hazelnut trees, which serve as inoculated hosts for the truffle mycorrhiza through long-term stewardship.

Puget Sound shell inputs

Annual liming with upcycled oyster shells keeps soils alkaline for truffle production while sustaining nut yield and resilience.

Regenerative discipline

Biology-forward orchard care informed by mycology, permaculture practice, and shell-based mineral amendments.

Symbiosis & codependence

Hazelnuts and Périgord black truffles thrive together through deliberate partnership.

Sugars to the fungi

The hazelnut roots provide plant-derived carbohydrates essential for truffle formation because the trees themselves are the inoculated hosts.

Nutrients to the trees

The truffle mycorrhiza increases nutrient uptake, improves soil structure, and boosts tree health.

Shell-powered balance

Annual liming with upcycled oyster shells maintains the alkaline soil conditions needed for truffles while reinforcing hazelnut vigor.

Shared terroir

An ecosystem where outputs become inputs, binding orchard and Puget Sound influence into one distinctive flavor profile.

Farmstead vision

A working orchard and gathering place shaped by craftsmanship, ecology, and Puget Sound influence.
