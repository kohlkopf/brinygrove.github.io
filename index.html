Regenerative hazelnuts and truffles on Washington's coastal edge.

Briny Grove Farmstead is a 10-acre regenerative orchard in Washington State centered on a unique marriage of hazelnut cultivation and Périgord black truffle production. The farm is built around a circular, sea-to-soil philosophy that transforms waste oyster shells from Puget Sound into a valuable soil input—closing loops, reducing waste, and creating a maritime-influenced terroir.

Hazelnut + truffle orchard

A resilient, dual-purpose planting crafted for long-term canopy health, fungal vigor, and coastal terroir.

Hazelnuts

Cold-hardy European hazelnuts selected for disease resistance, productivity, and flavor—laid out for stable root zones, runoff control, and fungal health.

Périgord truffles

Tuber melanosporum nurtured beneath the trees through careful inoculation and long-term mycorrhizal stewardship.

Coastal inputs

Annual liming with valorized oyster shells keeps soils alkaline for truffle production while sustaining nut yield and resilience.

Regenerative discipline

Biology-forward orchard care informed by mycology, permaculture practice, and shell-based mineral amendments.

Symbiosis & codependence

Hazelnuts and Périgord black truffles thrive together through deliberate partnership.

Sugars to the fungi

The hazelnut roots provide plant-derived carbohydrates essential for truffle formation.

Nutrients to the trees

The truffle mycorrhiza increases nutrient uptake, improves soil structure, and boosts tree health.

Shell-powered balance

Annual liming with upcycled oyster shells maintains the alkaline soil conditions needed for truffles while reinforcing hazelnut vigor.

Shared terroir

An ecosystem where outputs become inputs, binding orchard and coast into one distinctive flavor profile.
